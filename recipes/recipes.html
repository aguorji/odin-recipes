Description

This hearty vegetable soup is a perfect comfort food for any season. It’s made with a blend of fresh, vibrant vegetables like carrots, celery, tomatoes, and onions, simmered slowly to bring out their natural flavors. The broth is rich and savory, with hints of garlic and herbs like thyme and rosemary, making each spoonful feel like a warm hug. Whether served with a slice of crusty bread or enjoyed on its own, this soup is a nutritious and satisfying meal that can be enjoyed by all ages.

Packed with vitamins and minerals, this vegetable soup is not only delicious but also a healthy option for those looking to add more plant-based meals to their diet. The combination of colorful vegetables and the wholesome broth makes it light yet filling, while the spices give it just the right amount of depth. It's an ideal choice for those seeking a gluten-free, dairy-free, or low-calorie meal that doesn’t compromise on taste.

Ingredients

  • Carrots
  • Celery
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Olive oil
  • Vegetable broth
  • Fresh thyme
  • Fresh rosemary
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Bay leaves

Steps

  1. Prepare the vegetables:
    • Peel and dice the carrots.
    • Chop the celery, onions, and tomatoes.
    • Mince the garlic.
  2. Sauté the aromatics:
    • In a large pot, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat.
    • Add the onions and garlic, sautéing until they become soft and translucent (about 5 minutes).
  3. Add the vegetables:
    • Add the diced carrots, celery, and chopped tomatoes to the pot.
    • Stir everything together and cook for another 5 minutes to allow the vegetables to soften slightly.
  4. Add the broth and spices:
    • Pour in the vegetable broth (or chicken broth) to cover the vegetables.
    • Add a couple of bay leaves, fresh thyme, and rosemary. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Simmer the soup:
    • Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 25-30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Adjust seasoning:
    • Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with more salt, pepper, or herbs if needed.
  7. Serve:
    • Remove the bay leaves and serve the soup hot.
    • Optionally, pair with a slice of crusty bread for dipping.
